Why Now: Market Timing Factors

01
Alert Fatigue Crisis
70%+ of alerts go uninvestigated. Security tools have become their own operational burden. Buyers desperate for prioritization.
02
The "Wiz Hangover"
Organizations bought Wiz for visibility. Now drowning in theoretical vulnerabilities. Next wave of spend goes to context—Upwind's thesis.
03
eBPF Maturity
eBPF reached stability in Linux kernels 2018-2020. Now enterprise-ready. Technology risk largely resolved.
04
Kubernetes Ubiquity
70%+ enterprises now use Kubernetes. Container adoption continues growing (50% by 2029). Addressable footprint expands continuously.
05
Regulatory Pressure
SEC's 4-day breach disclosure rules (2023) increase urgency. Boards care about cyber risk in unprecedented ways.
06
Consolidation Demand
75% of organizations want fewer security tools (Gartner). Comprehensive platforms beat point solutions.
07
Wiz-Google Disruption
AWS and Azure customers nervous about Google-owned security vendor. 12-18 month window for alternatives to capture displaced demand.
★
Convergence Window
All 7 factors converging simultaneously. This is the optimal moment for Upwind's runtime-first approach.

Opportunities for Upwind
  • Cloud neutrality concerns: AWS and Azure-primary enterprises prefer independent vendor
  • Innovation slowdown: Google integration typically absorbs management attention
  • Customer displacement: Enterprises actively evaluating alternatives
  • 12-18 month window before integration concerns fade
Risks to Monitor
  • Google resources accelerate Wiz's capability development
  • Google distribution could extend Wiz's reach
  • Bundling and discounting could pressure pricing
  • Displacement window is time-limited
